News & Updates

Welcome to the October Coast Summer Art Camp! Running throughout the summer of 2026 in Berwyn, IL, our camp is designed to keep creativity alive while school is out. [span_0](start_span)We keep costs incredibly low by utilizing upcycled materials, bulk basics, and natural items[span_0](end_span). Check back here for weekly updates and supply drives!

The 10-Week "Summer of Creation" Curriculum

Week 1: Cardboard City (Architecture & Engineering)

[span_1](start_span)

Theme: Building 3D structures out of recycled boxes[span_1](end_span).

    [span_2](start_span)
  • Ages 5-6: Painting large appliance boxes to make "houses" and crawling through them[span_2](end_span).
    • [span_3](start_span)
  • Ages 7-12: Constructing miniature cities, dioramas, or marble runs using toilet paper rolls, shoeboxes, and masking tape[span_3](end_span).

Week 2: Nature's Palette (Eco-Art)

[span_4](start_span)

Theme: Using the outdoors as both inspiration and material[span_4](end_span).

    [span_5](start_span)
  • Ages 5-6: Mud painting, leaf rubbings with unwrapped crayons, and collecting twigs[span_5](end_span).
    • [span_6](start_span)
  • Ages 7-12: Andy Goldsworthy-inspired temporary land art, making paint out of crushed berries/spices, and sun prints (cyanotypes)[span_6](end_span).

Week 3: Messy Masterpieces (Process Art)

[span_7](start_span)

Theme: Focusing on how art is made rather than the final product[span_7](end_span).

    [span_8](start_span)
  • Ages 5-6: Bubble painting (blowing bubbles in paint and catching them on paper), finger painting, and sponge stamping[span_8](end_span).
    • [span_9](start_span)
  • Ages 7-12: Jackson Pollock-style splatter painting outdoors, pendulum painting, and tape-resist geometric canvas art[span_9](end_span).

Week 4: The Puppet Theater (Storytelling)

[span_10](start_span)

Theme: Creating characters and bringing them to life[span_10](end_span).

    [span_11](start_span)
  • Ages 5-6: Paper bag puppets and simple sock puppets[span_11](end_span).
    • [span_12](start_span)
  • Ages 7-12: Papier-mâché marionettes or shadow puppets using black cardstock and a white sheet illuminated by a flashlight[span_12](end_span).

Week 5: Cultural Crafts (World Art)

[span_13](start_span)

Theme: Exploring techniques from around the globe[span_13](end_span).

    [span_14](start_span)
  • Ages 5-6: Easy tissue-paper "stained glass" and simple origami[span_14](end_span).
    • [span_15](start_span)
  • Ages 7-12: Making miniature piñatas from recycled cereal boxes, weaving with yarn on cardboard looms, or Mexican folk art mirrors[span_15](end_span).

Week 6: Wild Wearables (Fashion & Jewelry)

[span_16](start_span)

Theme: Art you can wear[span_16](end_span).

    [span_17](start_span)
  • Ages 5-6: Macaroni necklaces, paper crowns, and superhero masks[span_17](end_span).
    • [span_18](start_span)
  • Ages 7-12: Tie-dyeing old white t-shirts, creating complex friendship bracelets, or designing custom tote bags with fabric markers[span_18](end_span).

Week 7: 2D Explorations (Drawing & Comic Books)

[span_19](start_span)

Theme: Mastering pencils, pens, and paper[span_19](end_span).

    [span_20](start_span)
  • Ages 5-6: Collaborative mural drawing on giant rolls of butcher paper[span_20](end_span).
    • [span_21](start_span)
  • Ages 7-12: Designing their own comic books, learning basic anatomy, and practicing continuous contour drawing[span_21](end_span).

Week 8: Junk Drawer Sculptures (Upcycling)

[span_22](start_span)

Theme: Turning trash into treasure[span_22](end_span).

    [span_23](start_span)
  • Ages 5-6: Gluing bottle caps, buttons, and pipe cleaners to heavy cardboard[span_23](end_span).
    • [span_24](start_span)
  • Ages 7-12: Creating "robot" sculptures using tin cans, wire, old CDs, and hot glue (supervised)[span_24](end_span).

Week 9: Under the Sea (Water & Color)

[span_25](start_span)

Theme: Aquatic life and watercolor techniques[span_25](end_span).

    [span_26](start_span)
  • Ages 5-6: Watercolor and salt painting, creating egg-carton sea turtles[span_26](end_span).
    • [span_27](start_span)
  • Ages 7-12: Wet-on-wet watercolor landscapes, wax resist painting (white crayon under watercolor), and 3D paper fish mobiles[span_27](end_span).

Week 10: The Camp Gallery (Exhibition)

[span_28](start_span)

Theme: Curating and displaying art[span_28](end_span).

    [span_29](start_span)
  • All Ages: This week is spent finishing up favorite projects, creating "artist statements," and setting up a gallery[span_29](end_span). [span_30](start_span)On Friday, invite parents to walk through the space to see what the kids created[span_30](end_span)!
