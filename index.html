Welcome to Camp

Welcome to the October Coast Summer Art Camp! Running throughout the summer of 2026 in Berwyn, IL, our camp is designed to keep creativity alive while school is out.

10-Week Curriculum

Our "Summer of Creation" explores a different theme every week. Kids are split into two age groups (5-6 and 7-12) to ensure projects are engaging and age-appropriate.

Week 1: Cardboard City (Architecture)

  • Ages 5-6: Painting large appliance boxes to make "houses" and crawling through them.
  • Ages 7-12: Constructing miniature cities, dioramas, or marble runs using upcycled boxes and tape.

Week 2: Nature's Palette (Eco-Art)

  • Ages 5-6: Mud painting, leaf rubbings with unwrapped crayons, and twig collecting.
  • Ages 7-12: Andy Goldsworthy-inspired temporary land art, crushed berry paint, and sun prints (cyanotypes).

Week 3: Messy Masterpieces (Process Art)

  • Ages 5-6: Bubble painting, finger painting, and sponge stamping.
  • Ages 7-12: Jackson Pollock-style splatter painting outdoors, pendulum painting, and tape-resist geometric art.

Week 4: The Puppet Theater (Storytelling)

  • Ages 5-6: Paper bag puppets and simple sock puppets.
  • Ages 7-12: Papier-mâché marionettes or shadow puppets using black cardstock.

Week 5: Cultural Crafts (World Art)

  • Ages 5-6: Easy tissue-paper "stained glass" and simple origami.
  • Ages 7-12: Miniature recycled piñatas, yarn weaving on cardboard looms, or Mexican folk art mirrors.

Week 6: Wild Wearables (Fashion)

  • Ages 5-6: Macaroni necklaces, paper crowns, and superhero masks.
  • Ages 7-12: Tie-dyeing old t-shirts, complex friendship bracelets, or custom tote bags.

Week 7: 2D Explorations (Drawing)

  • Ages 5-6: Collaborative mural drawing on giant rolls of butcher paper.
  • Ages 7-12: Designing comic books, basic anatomy, and continuous contour drawing.

Week 8: Junk Drawer Sculptures (Upcycling)

  • Ages 5-6: Gluing bottle caps, buttons, and pipe cleaners to heavy cardboard.
  • Ages 7-12: Creating "robot" sculptures using tin cans, wire, old CDs, and hot glue.

Week 9: Under the Sea (Water & Color)

  • Ages 5-6: Watercolor and salt painting, egg-carton sea turtles.
  • Ages 7-12: Wet-on-wet watercolor landscapes, wax resist painting, and 3D paper fish mobiles.

Week 10: The Camp Gallery (Exhibition)

  • All Ages: Finishing up favorite projects, creating "artist statements," and setting up a gallery. On Friday, parents are invited to walk through the space to see what the kids created!

Medical & Emergency Contact Policy

Parents must disclose if their child has any allergies (Food, environmental, medical - Note: We use recycled food containers like egg cartons in our crafts). Parents must also disclose if the child has any medical conditions, special needs, requires any accommodations, or carries an EpiPen or Inhaler.

Consent for Emergency Medical Treatment: In the event of a medical emergency, I grant permission to October Coast Summer Art Camp staff to administer basic first aid. If I or my designated emergency contacts cannot be reached, I authorize staff to secure emergency medical care, including calling 911 or transporting my child to the nearest hospital. I agree to assume full financial responsibility for any emergency medical care provided.

Food, Snack, and Allergy Waiver

Lunch & Water Policy: I understand that October Coast Summer Art Camp does not provide full meals. I agree to send my child with a packed, nut-free lunch and a filled, reusable water bottle each day.

Provided Snacks: October Coast Summer Art Camp will provide a daily light snack (e.g., pretzels, fruit, crackers, or popsicles). Parents will designate whether they give permission or do not give permission for their child to consume camp-provided snacks. If no permission is given, parents must pack a separate snack each day.

Allergy Acknowledgment: I understand that while October Coast Summer Art Camp aims to be a nut-free environment, they cannot guarantee a facility entirely free of allergens, especially given the use of upcycled food boxes (like egg cartons or cereal boxes) in art projects.

During the course of the October Coast Summer Art Camp summer program, we will be taking photographs and videos of the campers creating art, as well as photographing their final art projects. These images may be used for promotional purposes, including our website, social media pages, and future community flyers.

Parents may grant full permission, permission for artwork only (no faces), or withhold all permission. If permission is granted, a child's first name will never be published alongside their photo.
