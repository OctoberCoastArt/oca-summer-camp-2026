// about the camp

Where Kids Make Real Art

October Coast Summer Art Camp is a hands-on, imagination-first art program rooted in the October Coast creative tradition. We believe every kid is already an artist — we just give them the materials, the space, and the permission to prove it.

Located in the Berwyn, IL area, our camp serves young artists ages 5 and up across the summer, from the second week of June through mid-August. Expect printmaking, painting, collage, photography, and whatever weird beautiful things emerge when you put art supplies in front of a curious kid.

🎨 What We Do

10 weeks of hands-on projects: sculpture, painting, eco-art, puppets, comics, tie-dye, watercolor, and more. Real materials. Real results. Real mess.

📅 Dates & Sessions

June 8 – August 14, 2026. 10 weekly themed sessions. Week 10 ends with a parent gallery walk — kids curate and display their own work.

📍 Location

Berwyn, IL area (Districts 98 & 100). Exact address provided upon registration confirmation.

👦 Who Can Come

Ages 5 and up, split into two groups: ages 5–6 and ages 7–12. No experience necessary — only curiosity required.

// 10-week curriculum

The Summer of Creation

Each week has its own theme and projects, split by age group. Materials are low-cost, upcycled, and nature-based wherever possible.

```

Week 1 — Cardboard City

Ages 5–6: Painting giant boxes, crawling through them.
Ages 7–12: Miniature cities, dioramas, and marble runs from recycled materials.

Week 2 — Nature's Palette

Ages 5–6: Mud painting, leaf rubbings, twig collecting.
Ages 7–12: Land art, berry-and-spice paint, sun prints (cyanotypes).

Week 3 — Messy Masterpieces

Ages 5–6: Bubble painting, finger painting, sponge stamping.
Ages 7–12: Splatter painting, pendulum painting, tape-resist canvas.

Week 4 — Puppet Theater

Ages 5–6: Paper bag and sock puppets.
Ages 7–12: Papier-mâché marionettes or shadow puppets with backlit screen.

Week 5 — Cultural Crafts

Ages 5–6: Tissue-paper stained glass, simple origami.
Ages 7–12: Mini piñatas, cardboard-loom weaving, folk art mirrors.

Week 6 — Wild Wearables

Ages 5–6: Macaroni necklaces, paper crowns, superhero masks.
Ages 7–12: Tie-dye shirts, friendship bracelets, fabric-marker tote bags.

Week 7 — Comics & Drawing

Ages 5–6: Collaborative mural on giant butcher paper.
Ages 7–12: Comic book design, basic anatomy, contour drawing.

Week 8 — Junk Sculptures

Ages 5–6: Gluing bottle caps, buttons, and pipe cleaners to cardboard.
Ages 7–12: Robot sculptures from tin cans, wire, old CDs, and hot glue.

Week 9 — Under the Sea

Ages 5–6: Watercolor and salt painting, egg-carton sea turtles.
Ages 7–12: Wet-on-wet watercolor, wax resist painting, 3D paper fish mobiles.

Week 10 — The Camp Gallery 🎉

All Ages: Finish favorite projects, write artist statements, and curate a gallery show. Friday: parents invited to walk through and celebrate.

```

Medical & Emergency Contact Form

At check-in, parents/guardians provide the following information on file:

Emergency Contacts: Two emergency contacts with name, relationship, primary phone, and alternate phone are required.

Medical Information: We collect information on food, environmental, and medical allergies (including note that we use upcycled food containers such as egg cartons in crafts); any medical conditions, special needs, or accommodations; and whether the child carries an EpiPen or inhaler with instructions.

Emergency Medical Consent: In the event of a medical emergency, parents grant permission to camp staff to administer basic first aid. If parents or emergency contacts cannot be reached, staff are authorized to secure emergency medical care, including calling 911 or transporting the child to the nearest hospital. Parents assume full financial responsibility for any emergency medical care provided.

Physical signature required at check-in. © 2026 October Coast Summer Art Camp

Food, Snack & Allergy Policy

Lunch & Water Policy: October Coast Summer Art Camp does not provide full meals. Families agree to send their child with a packed, nut-free lunch and a filled, reusable water bottle each day.

Provided Snacks: The camp provides a daily light snack (examples: pretzels, fruit, crackers, or popsicles). At check-in, families indicate either: permission for their child to consume camp-provided snacks, or a preference to pack a separate snack each day.

Allergy Acknowledgment: All food allergies must be listed on the Medical Form. While October Coast Summer Art Camp aims to maintain a nut-free environment, we cannot guarantee a facility entirely free of allergens — particularly given the use of upcycled food packaging (egg cartons, cereal boxes) in art projects.

Physical signature required at check-in. © 2026 October Coast Summer Art Camp

Photo & Media Release

During camp sessions, we photograph campers making art and photograph finished artwork. These images may be used for our website, social media, and community materials. A child's full name is never published alongside their photo. At check-in, families select one of three options:

Option A — Full Permission: Permission to photograph/record the child and use images in promotional materials. Full name will never be published.

Option B — Artwork Only: Permission to photograph the child's artwork only. No faces or identifying features in public materials.

Option C — No Permission: No photography of the child or their artwork for public use.

Physical signature required at check-in. © 2026 October Coast Summer Art Camp

Code of Conduct

October Coast Summer Art Camp is a creative, inclusive space. We expect all campers, families, and staff to treat one another with respect. Bullying, discrimination, or unsafe behavior will result in removal from the program without refund. We are committed to a safe, joyful, and creative environment for every child.

Refund & Cancellation Policy

Camp sessions cancelled by October Coast due to weather or unforeseen circumstances will be rescheduled or refunded in full. Cancellations by families made more than 7 days before a session start will receive a full refund. Cancellations within 7 days of a session start are non-refundable unless the camper is ill. We evaluate all situations with common sense and good faith — please reach out directly at info@octobercoast.art.

Operating License & Business Registration

October Coast LLC is registered in the State of Illinois. Business license and operating documentation will be posted here upon completion. Questions about our legal standing or structure can be directed to info@octobercoast.art.
